Best Books I Have Ever Read

Since I was very young, my dad never objected to buying a great book. He could say “no” to get some candies or Hotwheels, but he never said “no” to a book. He encouraged me to dive into books and let them teach me about life. Little I knew that these books would impact the way I see myself and my surroundings, configuring the way I behave and analyze complex situations. From Latin American literature to self-help books, this is the compilation I will always recommend reading.

  1. The Rebellion of the Rats

    Year of Publication: 1968

    Author: Fernando Soto Aparicio

    Synopsis: "The Rebellion of the Rats" (La Rebelión de las Ratas) is a novel by Colombian author Fernando Soto Aparicio. Set in Bogotá, Colombia, the novel portrays the struggle of impoverished workers against a corrupt and oppressive society. It delves into themes of social injustice, exploitation, and the quest for dignity.

  3. The Picture of Dorian Gray

    Year of Publication: 1890

    Author: Oscar Wilde

    Synopsis: "The Picture of Dorian Gray" is a philosophical novel by Oscar Wilde. It tells the story of a young man named Dorian Gray who remains youthful and beautiful while a portrait of him ages and reflects his moral corruption. The novel explores themes of vanity, hedonism, and the consequences of one's actions.

  4. Las Malas

    Year of Publication: 2019

    Author: Camila Sosa Villada

    Synopsis: "Las Malas" is a novel by Argentine author Camila Sosa Villada. The book follows the lives of transgender women who work as sex workers in the city of Córdoba, Argentina. It explores their struggles, relationships, and dreams, offering a powerful portrayal of resilience and survival in the face of societal prejudice and discrimination.

  5. Los Goles de Juancho

    Year of Publication: 2010

    Author: Margarita Londoño

    Synopsis: "Los Goles de Juancho" is a children's book by Colombian author Margarita Londoño. The story revolves around a young boy named Juancho who dreams of becoming a soccer star. Through determination and hard work, Juancho learns valuable lessons about teamwork, friendship, and the importance of chasing one's dreams.

  6. My Name is Stilton, Geronimo Stilton

    Year of Publication: 2000

    Author: Elisabetta Dami

    Synopsis: "My Name is Stilton, Geronimo Stilton" is the first book in the Geronimo Stilton series, written by Italian author Elisabetta Dami. It follows the adventures of Geronimo Stilton, a mouse who works as a journalist and editor for the fictional newspaper, The Rodent's Gazette. The series combines humor, adventure, and education, targeting young readers.

  7. Percy Jackson & The Lightning Thief

    Year of Publication: 2005

    Author: Rick Riordan

    Synopsis: "Percy Jackson & The Lightning Thief" is the first book in the Percy Jackson & The Olympians series by American author Rick Riordan. It follows the adventures of Percy Jackson, a young boy who discovers he is a demigod, the son of Poseidon, and embarks on a quest to prevent a war among the gods. The series blends elements of Greek mythology with contemporary settings and humor.

  8. Rich Dad, Poor Dad

    Year of Publication: 1997

    Author: Robert T. Kiyosaki

    Synopsis: "Rich Dad, Poor Dad" is a personal finance book by Robert T. Kiyosaki. It explores the author's upbringing with two father figures: his biological father (referred to as "poor dad") and the father of his childhood friend (referred to as "rich dad"). Through their differing financial philosophies and lessons, Kiyosaki imparts insights on building wealth, investing, and achieving financial independence.
