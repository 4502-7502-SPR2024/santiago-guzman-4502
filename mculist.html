  1. Iron Man 2

    Phase: 1

    Director: Jon Favreau

    Synopsis: Altough Whiplash was not the most advanced villian in the list, the character growth of Tony Stark was incredible, I think this development has been one of the most important ones in the storyline since he acknowledged that his dad set him up for a better purpose than just being a millionare. I loved the relation that grew with Pepper as well and the relation with War Machine.

    Phase: 3

    Synopsis: I remember going to the premier of this movie, I went with me whole group of friends that I have shared classes since I was 7 years old. We were doing the line to claim our tickets and the people were iin full costumes to go in the theatre, my favorite moment of this whole saga needs to be when Falcon communicates to Captain America that he is on his left.

  3. The Avengers

    Phase: 1

    Director: Joss Whedon

    Synopsis: The excitement leading up to the release of "The Avengers" was palpable. It was the culmination of years of individual superhero movies, promising an epic team-up like never before. Watching Earth's mightiest heroes assemble on the big screen for the first time was an unforgettable experience. The chemistry between the characters, the exhilarating action sequences, and the iconic shot of the Avengers circle were moments etched into the memories of every fan.

  4. Guardians of the Galaxy

    Phase: 2

    Director: James Gunn

    Synopsis: "Guardians of the Galaxy" brought a refreshing and unexpected twist to the MCU. The quirky characters, the vibrant soundtrack, and the cosmic setting made for a unique and thoroughly enjoyable movie experience. Rocket and Groot's dynamic stole the show, while Star-Lord's mixtape added a nostalgic touch. The film's humor and heartwarming moments resonated with audiences, making it an instant favorite.

  5. Captain America: The Winter Soldier

    Phase: 2

    Directors: Anthony Russo, Joe Russo

    Synopsis: "Captain America: The Winter Soldier" took the MCU in a thrilling new direction. The espionage thriller combined intense action with a gripping narrative, keeping viewers on the edge of their seats throughout. The revelation of the Winter Soldier's true identity and the betrayal within S.H.I.E.L.D. were shocking twists that left a lasting impact. Steve Rogers' struggle to adapt to the modern world and his unwavering dedication to doing what's right showcased the essence of Captain America.

  6. Avengers: Age of Ultron

    Phase: 2

    Director: Joss Whedon

    Synopsis: "Avengers: Age of Ultron" raised the stakes for Earth's mightiest heroes. The introduction of Ultron, a formidable AI villain, challenged the Avengers like never before. The film explored themes of hubris and redemption as Tony Stark's creation threatened to destroy humanity. New characters such as Vision and Scarlet Witch added depth to the ensemble cast, while the climactic battle against Ultron's army provided jaw-dropping spectacle.

  7. Black Panther

    Phase: 3

    Director: Ryan Coogler

    Synopsis: "Black Panther" broke new ground as the first MCU film to feature a predominantly black cast and a superhero of African descent. The fictional nation of Wakanda, with its advanced technology and rich culture, captivated audiences worldwide. T'Challa's journey to reclaim the throne and confront the sins of his father resonated deeply, while Killmonger emerged as one of the most compelling villains in the MCU. The film's exploration of identity, heritage, and responsibility left a profound impact on viewers.

  8. Thor: Ragnarok

    Phase: 3

    Director: Taika Waititi

    Synopsis: "Thor: Ragnarok" revitalized the Thor franchise with its bold aesthetic and irreverent humor. Director Taika Waititi infused the film with his signature style, resulting in a colorful and comedic adventure unlike any other in the MCU. Thor's journey of self-discovery, coupled with his unlikely alliance with the Hulk and his estranged brother Loki, made for an entertaining ride. The film's exploration of themes such as redemption and the destruction of Asgard added depth to its narrative, while memorable characters like Valkyrie and Korg left a lasting impression.
